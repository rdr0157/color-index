Proposal The purpose of this application is to assess the critical areas on the property identified as 1316 Mojonnier Road (APN 35060353004), located within the Rural Residential 5 (RR-5) zoning district. The proposed work involves grading and fill exceeding 3,500 cubic yards of material (B25-541), which exceeds the flexible threshold established in WWCC 18.04.070 and is subject to review under the State Environmental Policy Act (SEPA26-01). In addition, an in-ground pool has been proposed on the western portion of the property (B26-070). Findings of Fact The following Critical Areas are mapped as on or adjacent to the subject property: Critical Aquifer Recharge Areas (CARA) of High Recharge Vulnerability Geologically Hazardous Areas, including erosion (slopes greater than 15%) and seismic (moderate to high liquefaction susceptibility) On November 4, 2025, a Stop Work Order (C25-44) was posted on-site at 1316 Mojonnier Road, Walla Walla, WA 99362 (APN 350603530004), pursuant to WWCC 15.04.031 - Work Commencing Before Permit Issuance. On November 12, 2025, the applicant submitted Building Permit application B25-541 for grading and fill exceeding 3,500 cubic yards of material along the eastern and southern portions of the property On November 20, 2025, the Community Development Department, Planning Division, sent a Request for Information (RFI) to the applicant requiring revised plans showing the full extent of the grading, along with a critical areas permit application and associated report. On November 24, 2025, an initial building preview comment letter, prepared by SAFEbuilt (Washington Bellevue Office), was sent to the applicant requesting a geotechnical report, erosion control plan, and details regarding the total height of the walls and drainage mitigation. On November 25, 2025, the applicant responded to the letters referenced in the above findings. The submittal included a revised site plan and drawings depicting a terraced wall, but did not include the required erosion control plan or geotechnical report. The revised plans proposed a 4-foot retaining wall with a second 4-foot retaining wall above the finished grade, resulting in a total height of approximately 8 feet above the original grade. On December 3, 2025, a second building review comment letter, prepared by SAFEbuilt, was sent to the applicant requesting an erosion control plan and a geotechnical report. The letter further stated that a critical areas permit application and a report prepared by a qualified professional were required, consistent with a revised site plan depicting the full scope of work. On December 5, 2025, Planning staff and the County Building Official/Fire Marshal met with the applicant at the subject property. Planning staff reiterated what had been communicated multiple times in prior correspondence, stating that a geotechnical report was required due to the proximity of slopes exceeding 15%. Additionally, the Building Official stated that the report was necessary due to the compaction of fill material and if the retaining wall exceeded 5 feet in height. On December 9, 2025, Planning staff issued a third letter following the December 5 site visit. Staff reiterated that a critical areas permit application and report, as well as a SEPA application, were required. The letter further stated that, unless exempt under WAC 197-11-800, the flexible threshold listed in WAC 197-100-800(1)(a)(V) is 100 cubic yards, and that the raised threshold adopted by the County is 500 cubic yards, as noted in WWCC 18.04.070. On January 20, 2026, the applicant submitted a SEPA checklist and application, along with a “limited” geotechnical report. The report stated that the on-site slopes were less than 15% and that the retaining walls would be only 4 feet in height. The report did not include an analysis of the fill material, an erosion control plan, or details regarding the retaining walls. On January 27, 2026, a fourth building review comment letter, prepared by SAFEbuilt, was sent to the applicant requesting a revised geotechnical report, retaining wall details, a revised site plan, and an erosion control plan. On February 2, 2026, the applicant submitted a revised set of plans, a complete geotechnical report, retaining wall details, and an erosion control plan. On February 12, 2026, SAFEbuilt communicated that it had reviewed Building Permit application B25-541 for compliance with Appendix J of the 2021 International Building Code and recommended approval. The Notice of Application (NOA) with Optional Determination of Non-Significance (ODNS), as documented in the staff’s affidavit, was published in the Walla Walla Union-Bulletin, the County’s official gazette, on February 17, 2026. The notice was also posted on the Community Development Department (CDD) website, posted on-site, and distributed to the applicant and the reviewing agencies listed below. Washington State Department of Archaeology & Historic Preservation (DAHP) Department of Natural Resources (DNR) Department of Ecology (Eastern Region) Department of Fish and Wildlife (WDFW) Department of Transportation (South Central Region) Department of Agriculture Department of Commerce Department of Health (Rural Health) State Parks Walla Walla County Public Works Sheriffs Buildign Official / Fire Marshal GIS Department, Addressing Authority Department of Community Health, Environmental Health Division (Environmental Health) Municipalities located within Walla Walla County City of College Place City of Prescott City of Waitsburg City of Walla Walla Local Agencies Walla Walla Valley Metropolitan Planning Organization (WWVMPO) Port of Walla Walla Walla Walla County Conservation District Williams Pipeline Pacific Power Cascade Natural Gas Valley Transit No public comments were received during the notice period. Agency comments are summarized below. State agency letters were received via email, while County department comments were submitted through ComDev, the County’s permit tracking software. Walla Walla County Addressing Authority recommended approval on February 24, 2026, with no additional comments. Walla Walla County Public Works recommended approval on February 24, 2026, with the following comments: Access is not required All stormwater must remain on-site with no off-site discharges to the road right-of-way or adjacent parcels A letter from Amanda Hiebert, SEPA Coordinator for the Washington State Department of Ecology, Eastern Region Office, dated March 2, 2026: Included a comment from Chad Suave of the Water Quality Program noting that a Construction Stormwater General Permit is required for construction sites that disturb one acre or more of the total area and have, or will have, a discharge of stormwater to a surface water or storm sewer. On February 26, 2026, the applicant submitted Building Permit application B26-070 for an in-ground pool on the western portion of the property. The pool, which is at grade with the proposed fill on the eastern portion of the property, was originally identified in the initial notice as a “future project”. Plans were submitted separately to the Environmental Health Department which depicted the approximate septic system location and a proposed “pool house/ADU” within the graded area. On March 10, 2026, Planning staff issued a fourth letter conveying comments from the reviewing agencies and stating that a critical areas permit application and a revised site plan showing the full scope of work were required. Staff emphasized the inconsistent information submitted across multiple applications and noted that SEPA must evaluate the cumulative impacts of the full proposal, as reflected in a plan showing the full scope of work. On March 12, 2026, the applicant submitted separate plans to both the Community Development Department and the Environmental Health Department. The revisions submitted to Environmental Health depicted an updated septic system and reserve area location. Environmental Health recommended approval, noting that no additional soil is to be placed over the existing drainfield, which was identified during a site evaluation conducted on March 6, 2026. On March 13, 2026, the applicant met with Planning staff at the Community Development Department office to define the full scope of work and identify the necessary corrections to the site plan. On March 16, 2026, the applicant submitted a final revised site plan depicting the full extent of the grading, the location of the septic system and drainfield, and the proposed pool, and removing references to an ADU/pool house. On March 30, 2026, Planning staff sent an affidavit to the applicant to affirm that the submitted plan and materials represent the full scope of the proposed project and all activities anticipated within the next one (1) year. On March 30, 2026, the applicant submitted the signed and notarized affidavit, acknowledging that any expansion, modification, or omission of the project scope within that time may require additional review, public notice, fees, enforcement action, or other penalties authorized by law, and further agreeing to comply with all applicable regulations, codes, and conditions imposed as part of the review or approval of this application. The revised geotechnical report was prepared by GEOCON and is dated January 16, 2026, with the full referenced materials submitted as part of an updated submittal on January 20, 2026. The report was submitted for the grading permit, the scope included the placement, fill, and compaction of materials, along with the two-tier retaining walls, which are identified in the report as having an exposed height of 4 feet each. The report is limited to this proposal, and future development within this portion of the property may require additional reporting. However, as allowed under WWCC 18.08.530(D), the geotechnical report and its findings may be incorporated into future studies.
