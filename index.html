Full Session Description, including objectives (600) Permit and project application expectations can be difficult to understand for applicants unfamiliar with the development process, often resulting in incomplete or insufficiently detailed applications. Traditional online GIS portals are often not intuitive and are commonly designed for staff or technical users rather than the typical applicant. Walla Walla County is covered with critical areas and applicants were often surprised by additional requirements after submitting a building permit or land use application. This created frustration for applicants and additional review work for staff. Walla Walla County developed the Parcel Information Report (PIR), a public-facing tool that allows applicants to search by address or parcel number and generate a summary of relevant parcel information specific to their property. The tool brings together enterprise GIS resources, open-source components, and publicly available geospatial data to help applicants, property owners, and planning staff better understand potential permitting considerations before an application is submitted. This session will discuss why applicants may struggle to provide a complete application, how planning departments can identify and organize relevant data sources, and how GIS can be used for more than making maps . The presentation will focus on practical lessons learned from building the PIR, including data preparation, public-facing design, and interagency and interdepartmental coordination. It is especially relevant for small and medium-sized jurisdictions that may not have access to out-of-the-box GIS solutions but do have access to GIS tools, web platforms, and public data sources. Specifically, the session will guide attendees in how to: -Identify common reasons applicants submit incomplete or insufficient materials. -Understand how to locate, evaluate, and create parcel, zoning, critical areas, and permitting data into a single public-facing tool. -Recognize how common GIS platforms and open-source tools can be used to support planning, permit intake, and public communications -Employ low-cost, scalable solutions that improve public understanding of permitting requirements and complete application expectations. This session directly supports Track 4: Skills Building by providing practical,GIS / Technology-based strategies that planners can apply to improve public communication, development review. The session also aligns with AICP Core Competencies 2.e, Technology and Related Applications; 2.f, Spatial Analysis; and 7.g, Technical Proficiency, by demonstrating how planners can use evolving technology, open-source and enterprise spatial analysis, and technical innovation to advance practical planning objectives. Planning Director Melissa Shumake, AICP, will help connect the project to broader planning practice, while Assistant Planner Roman Rangel will discuss the technical development and implementation of the Parcel Information Report.
