Proposal The purpose of this Home Occupation application is to allow the applicant to operate a remote service business focused on the installation of solar electric and solar hot water systems. The site is located generally at 785 Tumbleweed Lane, Burbank, WA 99323, identified as Assessor Parcel Number (APN) 310926120001, within the Rural Agriculture 10 (RA-10) zoning district. Decision The application, HO26-01 for Hot Solar Solutions LLC C/O Troy Woody is hereby approved with conditions based on the following criteria for approval, findings of fact, and conclusions of law Findings of Fact On December 18, 2023, a Type I Home Occupation Permit (HO13-31034) was approved for Hot Solar Solutions LLC, c/o Troy Woody. On June 17, 2020, a Type II Home Occupation Permit (HO20-002) was approved, renewing and modifying the previous permit for Hot Solar Solutions LLC, C/O Troy Woody. This modification expanded the approved interior area from 200 to 800 square feet and allowed for two non-resident employees on-site. On December 9, 2025, a Notice of Required Action (C25-46) was sent to the applicant for the unpermitted home occupation at 785 Tumbleweed Lane, Burbank, WA 99323. Pursuant to WWCC 17.08.261(H)(1), a home occupation permit shall be revoked if the applicant or operator relocates their residence off-site. Because the business had relocated without notifying the County, it was operating without the required approvals and was therefore subject to WWCC 15.04.031 - Work Commencing Before Permit Issuance, which requires an investigation fee equal to the standard permit fee set forth in Chapter 3.08, resulting in a double permit fee. On January 12, 2026, the applicant submitted Home Occupation application HO26-01 to operate a remote service business focused on the installation of solar electric and solar hot water systems. The business will be primarily located within a 696-square-foot area of an existing barn, as well as within a 20,000-square-foot (100' x 200') layout area adjacent to the barn. One non-resident employee will be on-site, and no customer visits are planned. Pursuant to WWCC 17.16.014 - Permitted Uses Table, a Home Occupation, Type II is allowed as an administrative conditional use in the RA-10 zoning district, subject to the review process and requirements set forth in WWCC 17.08.261 and Chapter 17.40. The proposal satisfies the requirements of WWCC 17.08.261. The use is located outside an urban growth area, no customer visits are anticipated, and the use is not described in WWCC 17.08.261(J). One non-resident employee is anticipated on-site, together with a 20,000 square foot (100' x 200') exterior layout area and an interior space of 696 square feet. The exterior use, as described, is anticipated to be obstructed from view without the need for additional screening. In addition to the requirements of WWCC 17.08.261 - Home Occupation, Type II, the proposal must comply with WWCC Chapter 17.40 - Conditional Uses, including the decision criteria listed in WWCC 17.40.020(A) through (F). Pursuant to WWCC 17.40.020(A), the use must not endanger the public health or safety. Staff find that the proposal does not endanger public health or safety and presents minimal risk, provided it complies with all applicable access, health, building, fire, septic, and other regulations and standards applicable to any aspect of the project. This permit includes specific Conditions of Approval to ensure that the approved use satisfies, and remains in compliance with, this approval criterion. Pursuant to WWCC 17.40.020(B), the use must not generate significant nuisance conditions such as noise, dust, glare, and/or vibrations. Staff find that the proposal is not expected to generate significant nuisance conditions related to noise, dust, glare, or vibrations, provided the use is implemented as proposed in the submitted application materials. The scope of the approved use does not include loud mechanical equipment or nighttime activities that could generate noise or light impacts that could be a nuisance to adjacent properties. Pursuant to WWCC 17.40.020(C), the use must meet all required conditions and standards set forth in the district where it is proposed to be located. Staff find that the proposal satisfies the applicable conditions and standards of the RA-10 zoning district. This Home Occupation permit is required to operate a remote service business focused on the installation of solar electric and solar hot water systems. The proposed interior and exterior uses are situated and designed to satisfy applicable development standards, including those set forth in WWCC 17.08.261 - Home Occupation, Type II. Pursuant to WWCC 17.40.020(D), the use must be compatible with and consistent with the surrounding area. Staff find the proposal to be compatible with and consistent with the surrounding area. The subject property is approximately 40 acres in size, with no development to the north between the site and the Snake River. Neighboring properties are similar in size and are sparsely populated. Conditions of Approval are included to ensure that the business is maintained and operated in a manner compatible with the character of the surrounding area. Should exterior storage become a nuisance or visible, additional screening may be required. Pursuant to WWCC 17.40.020(E), the use must be compatible and consistent with the character of the area in which it is located. Staff find the proposed home occupation to be consistent with the goals and policies of the Walla Walla County Comprehensive Plan. The subject property is designated Rural Agriculture and is zoned RA-10. Section 6.3.6 of the Comprehensive Plan includes goals and policies intended to protect agricultural lands while allowing other compatible uses. Specifically, Goal RL-03 emphasizes providing opportunities to strengthen the economic well-being of rural areas through home-based occupations designed to serve the communities in which they are located. Pursuant to WWCC 17.40.020(F), the use must be supported by adequate public facilities or services. Staff find the proposal to be adequately supported by public facilities and services.
