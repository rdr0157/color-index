Proposal Hassler-McDonald 3-Lot BLA is a proposal to reconfigure the boundaries of three legal lots identified as Assessor’s Parcel Numbers (APNs) 350602420049, 350602410069, and 350602420045. The adjustment would consolidate the three existing lots, identified as Lots A through C, into two resulting lots, identified as Lots 1 and 2. The adjustment would better align the properties with established agricultural and fence boundaries. The site is located generally east of McDonald Road, between Detour and Frog Hollow Roads, within the Agriculture Residential 10 (AR-10) zoning district. Decision The application, BLA26-05 for Greta Hassler is here by approved with conditions based on the following criteria for approval, findings of fact, and conclusions of law. Findings of Fact The application BLA26-05 was submitted on March 12, 2026. The application was determined to be complete for processing on March 23, 2026. The BLA was sent to consulting agencies for comments on March 23, 2025. The purpose of this BLA is to reconfigure the boundaries of three legal lots identified as Assessor’s Parcel Numbers (APNs) 350602420049, 350602410069, and 35060240045. The subject properties are located within the AR-10 zoning district, where the minimum lot width is 330 feet and the minimum lot area is 10 acres. As described in the findings above, both parcels meet the dimensional standards set forth in WWCC 17.18. Future development on the adjusted parcels will be subject to the applicable provisions of Chapter 18.08 in effect at the time of review. The subject properties are located within a Critical Aquifer Recharge Area (CARA) of high recharge vulnerability. These areas are regulated under WWCC 18.08.220-270, and residential uses do not require a critical areas report pursuant to WWCC 18.08.220(A). The subject properties are located north of and adjacent to Mud Creek, a Fish and Wildlife Habitat Area regulated under WWCC 18.08.600-675. The minimum riparian habitat buffer required for Mud Creek is 50 feet from the ordinary high-water mark (OHWM), as specified in WWCC 18.08.650 (Table 4). Portions of the site are located within the Mud Creek mapped Zone A (100-year, or 1 percent annual chance) floodplain. These areas are regulated under WWCC Chapter 18.12, and unless specifically exempt, future development within these areas must be sited, at a minimum, outside the floodway and elevated at least one foot above the Base Flood Elevation (BFE) established in the FEMA Flood Study. County staff estimates this elevation to be between 513 and 531 feet above sea level (NGVD 1929); building permits within these areas would require an elevation certificate prior to approval. Wetlands are mapped on or near the subject properties, generally aligning with Mud Creek. These mapped wetlands, which are regulated under WWCC 18.08.300-350, represent approximate locations intended to serve as a guide for the County, project applicants, and property owners. Unless specifically exempt, development within wetlands and their associated buffers is prohibited, and any proposal for buffer averaging or reduction requires a mitigation plan prepared by a qualified professional, such as a biologist. The subject properties are located within mapped Fish and Wildlife Habitat Areas for both Ferruginous Hawks and Wintering Birds of Prey. Future development within these mapped critical areas may require a Critical Areas Report and a Critical Areas Permit application, consistent with the requirements of WWCC 18.08.630-650. Parcels 1 and 2 will have access to McDonald Road, a public road maintained by the County. Parcel 2 will have direct frontage, whereas access to Parcel 1 is to be obtained by an easement represented on Sheet 2 of the survey as C1 through C4. Pursuant to WWCC 16.70.010, a boundary line adjustment cannot create or diminish any easement; the survey denotes this easement as one to be created through a separate instrument, which has been added as a condition of approval. Conclusions of Law Pursuant to WWCC 17.18, the minimum lot size in the Agriculture Residential 10 (AR-10) zoning district is 10 acres, and the minimum lot width is 330 feet. As noted in Finding 12, the resulting lots meet these dimensional standards following adjustment. Approval of the BLA is in the public’s use and interest, as the resulting parcels remain consistent with the applicable zoning standards in the AR-10 zoning district and are better aligned with established agricultural fields. The proposed survey is in compliance with Revised Code of Washington Chapter 58.09. It has been reviewed by the County Surveyor. The proposed BLA, subject to the below conditions of approval, meets the criteria of WWCC Chapter 16.70 – Boundary Line Adjustments.
